Spies, Lies, and Cybercrime

Eric O'Neill · Apr '26

Wow, scary how we're all vulnerable in this online world. Big fan of Bitwarden after this!

Leonardo Da Vinci

Walter Isaacson · Mar '26

One of my absolute favourite picks! Engineer, Architecht, Artist, Surgeon...list goes on. Wow! only proves how curiosity & flow state could make one push thier limits, proving anything is possible

Million Dollar Weekend

Noah Kagan · Feb '26

My personal highly recommended book. Main Idea: Have a paying customer, take a deposit and then get to building. This solves for 3 core problems during building something new. 1: Your Idea is validated as someone is willing to already pay. 2: Accountability created to deliver the promised product / service. 3: Action > Idea

Show Your Work!

Austin Kleon · Jan '26

Quick read. Core Takeaway: What and why are you hiding your work, that you've put your efforts into buidling / making? Push it out into the world. Let people know, build credibility, trust and audience

Meditations

Marcus Aurelius · Mar '22

My Bible. My top most recommended book. First hand POV from the greatest emperor himself: Marcus Aurelius. What makes it more impactful is that all these pagese were the personal rumniations of the emperor and were never meant to be published. This makes it more valuable to see how despite having everything as an emperor all the excuses to pursue vices, get content by pursuing hedonism, he chose purpose over everything. Constant ruminating on the thought : "Momento Mori" - reminder of death
